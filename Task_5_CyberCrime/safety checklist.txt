STAY SAFE ONLINE: STUDENT CHECKLIST

1. The "PIN is for Sending" Rule (UPI Safety)
Always remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN or scan a QR code to receive money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "claim a prize" or "receive a refund," it is a scam.

2. Verify "Collect Requests" (UPI Safety)
Before approving any notification on apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, or Paytm, read the text carefully. Scammers often send "Collect Requests" hoping you’ll click "Pay" without looking.

3. Beware of "Digital Arrest" Scams
If you receive a video call from someone claiming to be from the CBI, Narcotics Bureau, or Police alleging that a package with illegal items has been found in your name—hang up. Genuine law enforcement agencies never "arrest" people over video calls or ask for "bail money" via transfer.

4. Use "Lock My Profile" on Social Media
Utilize the "Lock Profile" feature on platforms like Facebook and keep your Instagram private. This prevents strangers from viewing your photos or using your details for Identity Theft.

5. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking
Never log into your bank account or make a UPI payment on college or cafe Wi-Fi. Use your mobile data for any financial activity to prevent hackers from intercepting your data.

6. Check "Device Binding" Messages
If you receive an SMS with a code and a request to forward it to a specific number to "verify your account," do not forward it. This is a tactic used to hijack your UPI account by binding it to the scammer's device.

7. Spot Fake Job/Internship Offers
Be skeptical of "Part-time work from home" offers on WhatsApp or Telegram that ask you to "like YouTube videos" or "invest small amounts" for high returns. These are classic task-based scams.

8. Secure Your "Digital Locker"
Ensure your DigiLocker and Aadhaar (using the mAadhaar app) are protected with Biometric Locks. This prevents unauthorized access to your sensitive government documents if your phone is ever stolen.


HOW TO REPORT CYBERCRIME IN INDIA

A) If you have been defrauded or suspect a cybercrime, take action immediately:

B) National Cyber Crime Helpline: Dial 1930 (Available 24/7)

C) Official Reporting Portal: Visit cybercrime.gov.in

D) Bank Contact: Call your bank’s dedicated fraud department to block your cards or UPI ID.
