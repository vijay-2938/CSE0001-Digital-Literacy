THE DO'S:

      1.Turn on 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication): This is the single best thing you can do for your accounts. Even if your password gets leaked, no one can get in without that extra code on your phone. It takes two minutes to set up but saves a lot of headaches.

      2.Fact-check before you share: We see so much news on our feeds every day. Before hitting "repost," I always take a second to check if it’s from a real news source. Sharing fake news just makes you look bad, especially if you're trying to build a professional brand.

      3.Keep your LinkedIn updated: Treat your LinkedIn like a digital resume. Use a clear, professional photo and list your skills and projects at VIT Bhopal. It’s the first place recruiters look, so make sure it reflects who you are as a student.

      4.Give credit where it’s due: If you find a cool infographic or a helpful piece of code, don't just copy-paste it. Tag the creator or mention them. It’s not just polite; it’s how you build a good network in the tech community.

      5.Think before you hit 'Post': The internet is permanent. I try to ask myself: "Would I be okay with a future employer seeing this?" If the answer is no, it’s better to just keep it in the drafts.

THE DON'TS:

      1.Don’t overshare your life: Avoid posting your phone number, hostel room number, or pictures of your ID cards. It might seem harmless, but that’s exactly how identity theft and stalking start.

      2.Don’t feed the trolls: You’re going to run into people who just want to argue or be mean. Don't waste your energy replying to them. It’s much more professional to just block them or walk away from the screen.

      3.Don’t reuse the same password: I know it’s hard to remember a dozen different passwords, but using one "master password" for everything is a huge risk. If one site gets hacked, all your accounts are gone. Use a password manager instead.

      4.Don’t engage in toxic debates: Avoid getting into heated, disrespectful arguments about sensitive topics. One viral, angry comment can follow you for years and seriously hurt your career prospects later.

      5.Don’t click on random links: Whether it’s a "free gift card" or a suspicious "see who viewed your profile" link, just ignore it. These are almost always phishing traps designed to steal your login info.
